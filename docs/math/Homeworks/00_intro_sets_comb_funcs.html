HW 00: Sets, Combinatorics, Functions

Sets

01: Train/Test leakage

You collected 200 images. Train set \(T\) has 140 indices; Test set \(S\) has 40. A QA script flags overlap \(|T\cap S|=4\).

  1. Are the splits disjoint? If not, how many unique images actually exist across both sets?

  2. How many items must be moved to make \(T\) and \(S\) disjoint with target sizes \(140\) and \(40\) (no duplicates)? Propose a minimal-move strategy.

02: Two-annotator inclusion‚Äìexclusion

Out of 1,000 images, annotator \(R\) flags 260 as ‚Äúcat,‚Äù annotator \(S\) flags 310 as ‚Äúcat,‚Äù and both flag 190.

  1. How many images at least one annotator called ‚Äúcat‚Äù?

  2. How many images neither annotator called ‚Äúcat‚Äù?

03: Divisibility via inclusion‚Äìexclusion

Count the integers in \({1,\dots,100}\) divisible by \(5\) or \(7\). Show the inclusion‚Äìexclusion steps.

04: Inclusion‚Äìexclusion for surjections

Count surjective functions \(f:\{1,2,3,4\}\to\{a,b,c\}\) using inclusion‚Äìexclusion. Show your work.

Combinatorics

05: Augmentation pipeline counting

An image pipeline does: random crop (3 sizes), color jitter (on/off), horizontal flip (on/off), and chooses one of 4 background textures.

Assuming independence, how many distinct augmentation ‚Äúrecipes‚Äù can be produced for a given image?

06: Two hyperparameters ‚Äî combinations

Let hyperparameter set \(H_1\) have \(m\) options and \(H_2\) have \(n\) options.

  1. How many \((H_1,H_2)\) combinations are in a full grid search?

  2. Give a concrete example (e.g., \(H_1=\{\text{Adam},\text{SGD}\}\), \(H_2=\{10^{-3},10^{-4},10^{-5}\}\)) and compute the count.

07: Armenian car plates

State your assumptions about the plate format (e.g., DD LL DDD, digits 0‚Äì9, letters A‚ÄìZ, repetition allowed).

  1. Under your stated format, how many distinct plates exist?

  2. How does the answer change if letters cannot repeat inside the same plate?

08: Anagrams of ‚Äúcheese‚Äù

How many distinct words can you form by rearranging the letters of ‚Äúcheese‚Äù? (Handle repeated letters carefully.)

Functions

09: Real-world examples

Give one real example (and short justification) for each:

  • Injective (one-to-one, not necessarily onto)

  • Surjective (onto, not necessarily one-to-one)

  • Bijective (one-to-one and onto)

10: Counting small functions

How many functions \(f:\{1,2,3\}\to\{a,b\}\)?

How many are injective? How many are surjective? (Hint: total is \(2^3\).)

11: Even/odd ‚Äî activation symmetry

Classify each as even, odd, or neither, and briefly note the symmetry implication:

  • \(f(x)=x^2\) (parabola)

  • \(g(x)=x^3\)

  • \(h(x)=\tanh(x)\)

  • \(p(x)=\mathrm{ReLU}(x)=\max(0,x)\)

12: Function properties

Given \(f(x)=x+3x^2\), determine:

  • Is it injective or not?
  • Is it surjective or not?
  • Is it bijective or not?
  • What is the image of \(A\) under \(f\), when \(A=[-1,2]\)?
  • What is the preimage of \(B\) under \(f\), when \(B=[0,5]\)?

Briefly justify each answer.

13: Both even and odd

Describe all functions \(f:\mathbb{R}\to\mathbb{R}\) that are simultaneously even and odd. Prove your claim.
